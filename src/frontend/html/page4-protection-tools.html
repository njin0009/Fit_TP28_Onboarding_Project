📋 Application Guide — UV 9
  • Apply 20 minutes before going outdoors — sunscreen needs time to bind to skin
  • Reapply every 1.5 hours at UV 9 (standard is 2hrs, reduced at very high UV)
  • Reapply immediately after swimming, sweating, or towel drying regardless of timing
  • Use SPF 50+ at UV 9 — SPF 30 is insufficient at this level

Lightweight Long-Sleeve Shirt
At 28°C, choose breathable UPF-rated fabric. Long sleeves are recommended at UV 9+ to cover arms. Light colours reflect heat better.
UPF 50+ Long sleeve Linen or poly-blend Light colour
👖
Light Pants or Midi Skirt
Cover knees and calves. Loose-fit allows airflow at 28°C. Dark-wash denim provides natural UPF ~1700, but is too hot — opt for light linen or cotton blend instead.
Loose fit Linen or cotton Covers knees
🕶️
Wraparound Sunglasses
UV400 rating blocks 99–100% of UVA and UVB. Wraparound style prevents side exposure to the periorbital skin and eyes.
UV400 rating Wraparound style Polarised optional
💡 Comfort tip at 28°C: Prioritise breathable UPF-rated fabrics (linen, lightweight polyester) over dense materials. Loose-fitting, light-coloured clothing blocks UV radiation while maximising airflow — you don't need to sacrifice comfort for protection.
